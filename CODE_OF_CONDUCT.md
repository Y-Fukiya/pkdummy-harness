# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We want `pkdummy-harness` to be a practical, respectful place for improving synthetic PK-like workflow fixtures and related documentation.

Participants are expected to:

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Keep discussion focused on reproducible evidence, source traceability, and workflow safety.
- Avoid sharing private clinical data, proprietary datasets, secrets, or license-restricted tool artifacts.
- Clearly distinguish workflow fixtures from clinical inference, dose selection, or regulatory model qualification.

## Unacceptable Behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, personal attacks, discriminatory language, sharing confidential data, or encouraging unsafe clinical use of generated fixtures.

## Enforcement

Issues can be reported through GitHub issues, discussions, or private vulnerability reporting when the concern is safety-sensitive. Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments and may restrict participation when needed to protect the project and its users.

This code of conduct is intentionally short. If the project grows into a larger community, it can be replaced with a fuller policy such as the Contributor Covenant.
